The main challenges in learning

Neither at school nor at the institute do they teach us how to study the material properly. We prepare for exams and study tickets. We practice solving monotonous problems in order to pass the test better, but often in the end this does not give us real knowledge. Memorization quickly wears off and is not useful.

Conclusion: You have to know how to learn, too, but for some reason, they don't teach it very well. What can we do about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help to change the approach to learning, to make it effective and engaging.

Learning techniques

Five practices from Barbara Oakley.

  • The two types of attention

    Deeper knowledge comes from alternating between between hyper focus and scatter focus.

  • Recall

    Recalling what you've learned will allow you to connect the disparate portions of your memory.

  • Interleaving

    Learn multiple skills at the same time, they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    Come up with a good question that you really care about.

  • The illusion of competence

    Repeat material to yourself, write it down, tell a friend: you only think you have a grasp of the new topic.

Barbara Oakley's story

Since she was a child, Barbara had a problem with math. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not the smartest. In the army, she studied Russian to get a raise, and was so successful that she was promoted to commander. But to get promoted, she had to pass math. So Barbara came up with her own approach to the exact sciences. It turns out that if you're not good at something, your hard-won knowledge is much deeper than that of someone who can see everything at a glance.
